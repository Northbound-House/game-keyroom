THE THRESHOLD

The beacon burns behind you, throwing its old three-flash promise out along the line, and far out in the dark the tender Meridian has turned in toward it. She is coming — slow, dark, and trusting a light you only learned to make an hour ago.

Three stretches of black stand between her and the dock: the outer marker, the alignment, and the threshold itself. You keep the light for every one of them.

This time you are not getting out. You are the way in.
