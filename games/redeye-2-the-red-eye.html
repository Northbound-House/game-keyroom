Meridian Airways 077 · Somewhere over the Atlantic · Level II

THE RED-EYE

Cabin lights down. Forty souls asleep at nineteen thousand feet, and the drone of four engines swallowing every other sound.

Coralie Vance is not in 7B. Her seat is turned down, her scarf still folded on it — and tucked into your own seat pocket, in a hand you don't recognise, a single line:

"When the cabin sleeps, come to the purser's station. Bring what she gave you. — V"

Two trails run through this cabin. They meet at the teleprinter.

SENT AT DAWN

The teleprinter stutters, stops, and goes still. Five letters gone into the dark toward a newsroom that has been waiting eleven months for them.

V takes the seat beside you and, for the first time all night, stops pretending to work. "He wasn't hiding an affair. He was hiding a ledger — and she carried it through six countries in a hatbox because nobody searches a film star's luggage."

Behind her, the cabin sleeps on. Ahead, the first grey line of morning over Portugal — and a customs hall that does not yet know what is about to walk through it.
