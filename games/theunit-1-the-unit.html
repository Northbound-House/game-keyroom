THE UNIT

You paid four hundred dollars for a padlock someone else stopped paying for. That's the whole business: units go to auction when the rent stops, and the rent stops for ordinary reasons — a move, a divorce, a death.

But standing in it now, under a light that buzzes, this one is wrong. Nobody abandons a unit they were using last week. There's a phone in here. There's a tracker still pinging.

Somebody stopped paying eleven days ago, and everything in here says they didn't mean to stop anything.

LAST SEEN

You log the last place their data and the daylight agree on, and close the laptop on a stranger you now know better than some of your friends.

You keep telling yourself that's what it is. Carelessness.
