The wheels touch at first light and the cabin exhales. Somewhere over the Azores you stopped being a passenger.

Coralie Vance goes down the steps ten rows ahead of you in dark glasses, carrying nothing. V does not look at either of you. And waiting at the bottom of the airstairs, in a good coat, holding a photograph — your photograph — is a man from the airline's own security office.

The reel is in your pocket. The night desk holds the front page for another hour and a half.

This time you are not getting yourself out. You are getting it out.
